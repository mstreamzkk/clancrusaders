Game Summary

Clan Crusaders delivers light-hearted RTS gameplay on the go. Stunning artwork and a surreal story keep you entertained as you swipe your way to victory. The gameplay is quick to learn, but difficult to master!

The world of Clan Crusaders takes place on Erf, a radish-infested dimension created by the legendary Sorcerer Jimmy. Three dramatically different cultures, the Dragons, the Thieves, and the Knights, are pit against each other in a contest with the gravest consequences. The winner will be returned to their home in the Kingdom of Nob; the losers will be trapped in Erf with nothing but the radishes.

This is YOUR chance to take up arms and decide the doom or destiny of the world of Erf. Fight alongside Faction Leaders as a Kommandant to Escape from Erf! Use military tactics, surprise strategy, and old-fashioned luck to defeat Player or Computer opponents using the hexagonal, real-time, strategy system.

Dragons

Dragons in DTK are fat. They are fat and cute like potatoes. It is likely that the original dragons were not fat. However, untold years of sleeping has made our dragons plump and swollen. Because of this, they rely on mechanical augmentation. They have propellers to fly, for instance. They cannot breathe fire, but have developed a device, wearable on their immense bodies, which allows them to shoot jets of flame. They love gold and their favorite food is Knights (when attacking castles) and Thieves (when finding thieves in their lairs). Dragons are few in number but have great strength.

Thieves

In Erf, the King of Thieves still rules. He is old, very old, but the silver bells fixed to his ankles still shine like the moon (Jimmy put this cursed anklet on the King). Thieves live in a society where thievery is legal, and the norm. Most thieves have a professional trade like blacksmithing or carpentry and practices thievery to get whatever else they want.

Most thieves steal from knights and some thieves are brave enough to face the dragons.

Thieves are extremely short and very lithe. Thieves have developed the ability to turn their skin into the color of their surroundings. This active camouflage is the secret to their success. They shy away from pitched combat and prefer to fight their enemies using subterfuge and whoopie cushions.

The thieves' motivation is to steal the hoards of the dragons' and the knights' holy relics and then return to their home. They want to seal Erf off again, and the King of Thieves is determined to get the bells off his feet, or die trying!

Knights

Knights: Honor. Armor. And Huge Freaking Guns.

The Knights of Erf have had an amazing set of technological breakthroughs. They have retired their long swords and now wield assault rifles. They still favor the horse for its versatility on terrain and ride when they can, for their armor is heavy.

Picture Al from FullMetal Alchemist on a galloping horse shooting an AK-47.

Knights build big castles. Most of their hobbies are what you'd expect: rescuing princesses, torturing thieves in the dungeon, dueling, and shooting dragons. Unfortunately, there are no princesses, so they take turns dressing up like one and getting captured by other knights.

The Knights' greatest weakness is an attack to his honor. There are many mournful accounts of a knight sitting down to sup in his hall with the Princess (cross-dresser) he has saved, only to be embarrassed by a huge burst of flatulence from the whoopie cushion snuck under his seat by the thief.

Clan Crusaders - Story

Welcome to Erf, a pocket dimension created by the forgetful High Sorcerer Jimmy. In his home, Jimmy’s power is greater than the King of Nob himself. This power is enforced in no small measure by Jimmy’s Staff, a feared artifact capable of turning anyone – or anything into a radish. To protect his staff, Jimmy created Erf and stored his most valuable weapon there, in a radish field.

The powerful are always the target of the power-hungry. Three disparate factions, whose leaders have personal vendettas against the High Sorcerer, banded together to ambush and destroy the legendary Jimmy.

These factions, perhaps not so clever by themselves, managed to cross into the dimension of Erf by using ancient magic and the secret obelisk of Nob.

The Dragons, whose Queen hungers to restore the power of flight and firebreathing to her once great race (which Jimmy stripped them of), awaited in the mountains.

The Thieves, whose King Jimmy shackled with an unremovable string of silver bells when Jimmy found in stealing from his tower, hid in the forests.

The Knights, whose Commander had been turned into a monstrous toad, stood brazenly on the radish field itself.

They waited for the catalyst, the attack of Nemesor the Annoying on Jimmy, that would cause Jimmy to teleport to Erf to collect his terrifying Radish Staff.

But, when Jimmy appeared, their ambush failed. They underestimated the mind-bending powers of Nob Kingdom’s High Sorcerer. With a laugh and a half, Jimmy grabbed his staff, threw his pocket-lobster on the ground, and vanished in a spume of lobster bisque.

Then, clouds rolled in and a voice rolled out like thunder.

"To escape Erf, you must control all three obelisks on the island, the voice of Jimmy decreed. Only one of your clans will survive. The other two will be stranded here, in Erf, where my magic will slowly turn you into radishes."

In a flash, the three Clans turned on each other. After a brief quarrel about who deserved to live, they retreated to their bases to plot against one another.

This is where your journey begins, Kommandant.
